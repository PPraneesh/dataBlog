Normal Distribution

Normal distribution is a bell-shaped frequency distribution curve where most of the data values tend to cluster around the mean. This distribution is also known as ‘Guassian distribution’.

Properties of Normal Distribution :-

• The curve is bell-shaped.
• The curve is symmetrical.
• The random variable which is following normal distribution has equal mean, median and mode.
• The location of curve on graph is characterised by mean (μ).
• The spread of characterised by standard deviation (σ).
• The random variable has an infinite theoretical range – ∞ to + ∞ .

Importance of Normal distribution :-

• They give you an idea of what your data looks like.
• Properties in the real world such as the height,
the weight of individuals, and other natural properties can be represented by the normal distribution.
• The Normal distribution is also called the ‘Mother of all distribution’ because if we are not aware of the
nature of the distributions, we can assume that it follows the normal distribution.
